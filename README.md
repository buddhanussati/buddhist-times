## üåü Key Features

### ‚òÄÔ∏è Solar Noon & Meal Timer

The core feature of this application is the precise calculation of the allowable time for meal consumption (Dana) according to Vinaya rules.

* **Real-time Countdown:** Displays a live countdown timer to **Solar Noon** (Sun Zenith), not just 12:00 PM standard time.
* **Dawn to Noon Window:** Visually tracks the progress of the day from Dawn to Noon using a progress bar.
* **Status Indicators:** Clearly indicates if the current time is "Wait" (Pre-dawn), "Within meal time" (Dawn to Noon), or "Time's up" (Past Noon).
* **Solar Data:** Provides exact times for Dawn, Sunrise, Solar Noon, and Sunset based on your coordinates.

### üåï Uposatha Calculator

* **Next Uposatha Tracker:** Automatically calculates the upcoming Uposatha day based on the Lunar calendar.
* **Lunar Phase Identification:** Identifies whether the day is the 8th (Quarter), 15th (Full Moon), 23rd, or 30th (New Moon).
* **Visual Indicators:** Displays the corresponding Moon phase icon.

### üìç Location & Timezone

* **Dual Location Modes:**
1. **Automatic GPS:** Uses the browser's Geolocation API for the most accurate solar calculations based on your exact position.
2. **Manual City Selection:** A curated list of major cities, specifically optimized for Buddhist practitioners. Includes major hubs in:
* **Buddhist Pilgrimage Sites:** Bodh Gaya, Yangon, Mandalay, Mawlamyine (Pa-Auk).
* **Asia:** Bangkok, Sri Lanka, Vietnam, Singapore, Japan, etc.
* **Global:** Major cities in Europe, North/South America, and Australia.
* **Timezone Awareness:** Automatically adjusts calculations based on the selected city's timezone.

### üìÖ Calendar & Interface

* **Dual Dating:** Displays both the current **Gregorian Year** and the **Buddhist Era (BE)** year (Gregorian + 544).
* **Monastic Theme:** Designed with a "Saffron" and "Dark Mode" UI, optimized for low-light environments and battery saving.
* **Responsive Design:** Fully functional on mobile devices and desktops.

## üöÄ Usage

Simply open the `index.html` file in any modern web browser.

1. Grant location permissions for the most accurate solar noon calculation.
2. Alternatively, select your nearest city from the dropdown menu.
3. The app will store your last selected location in the browser's local storage for future visits.
